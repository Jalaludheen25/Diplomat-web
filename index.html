Why choose Dubai for starting a business?

So many reasons! Here's a list of the most common ones:

  • The UAE government actively promotes businesses as part of its long-term economic stability plan.
  • Dubai offers a tax-friendly environment and competitive labor and financing costs.
  • It has no foreign exchange controls, trade barriers, or quotas.
  • Dubai is strategically placed for doing business, with easy access to 1.5 billion consumers in Africa, West Asia, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East.
  • Our world-class transport links also make it an easy place to attract business to.
  • In terms of business setup, the process is straightforward and affordable.
  • Dubai has a large pool of highly educated talent and world-class infrastructure, making it ideal for startups.
  • Its high standard of living combined with unparalleled safety measures makes Dubai an alluring destination for global talent.
  • Conducting market research in Dubai is simplified due to clear procedures and robust legal frameworks.

How does the economy of Dubai support new businesses?

Dubai's economy is highly diversified and business-friendly, offering numerous advantages for new businesses including strategic location, world-class infrastructure, favorable tax policies, and access to global markets.

What are the steps to set up a business in Dubai?

The main steps include: 1) Choose your business activity and jurisdiction, 2) Reserve your company name, 3) Prepare required documentation, 4) Apply for business license, 5) Complete visa processing, 6) Open a corporate bank account. Company name can guide you through each step.

How can I determine the best location for my business in Dubai?

The best location depends on your business activity, target market, and operational needs. Free zones offer 100% foreign ownership and tax benefits, while mainland companies can operate anywhere in the UAE. Our experts can help you choose the optimal jurisdiction.

What are the differences between setting up a mainland business versus a free zone business?

Free zone businesses offer 100% foreign ownership, 0% corporate tax, and no customs duties, but are limited in where they can operate. Mainland businesses can operate anywhere in the UAE with unlimited business activities but may require a local partner depending on the business type.

How can business consultants or business setup consultants assist me?

Business setup consultants provide expert guidance through the entire company formation process, helping you choose the right jurisdiction, prepare documentation, navigate legal requirements, and ensure compliance with UAE regulations. They save you time and help avoid costly mistakes.

How can I open a corporate bank account for my company in Dubai?

Opening a corporate bank account requires your business license, Emirates ID, passport copies, business plan, and initial deposit. Different banks have varying requirements and processing times. Our banking specialists can help you choose the right bank and prepare all necessary documentation.

Are there any share capital requirements for specific business setups?

Yes, share capital requirements vary by jurisdiction and business type. Free zones typically have lower minimum capital requirements, while mainland companies may require higher capital depending on the business activity. Some activities have specific capital requirements set by regulatory authorities.

What is the process for obtaining employment visas and residence visas for staff?

The process involves obtaining work permits, medical examinations, Emirates ID applications, and residence visa stamping. Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks depending on the nationality and documentation. We handle the entire visa process for your employees.

What is the golden visa, and how does it differ from regular visas?

The Golden Visa is a long-term residence visa (5-10 years) for investors, entrepreneurs, and skilled professionals. Unlike regular visas, it offers extended validity, multiple entries, and doesn't require a sponsor. It's ideal for business owners looking for long-term residency in the UAE.

What are the visa processing times and associated fees?

Visa processing typically takes 2-4 weeks for employment visas and 1-2 weeks for investor visas. Fees vary by visa type, nationality, and processing speed. Express processing is available for urgent cases. We provide transparent pricing and timeline estimates for all visa services.

How do UAE free zones differ from one another?

Free zones differ in terms of permitted business activities, location, infrastructure, costs, and specific benefits. Some specialize in technology, others in trading or manufacturing. Each has unique advantages like proximity to airports, ports, or specific industry clusters.

What kind of business licenses are available in Dubai?

Dubai offers three main types of business licenses: Commercial (for trading activities), Professional (for service-based businesses), and Industrial (for manufacturing). Each license type has specific activities and requirements. The choice depends on your business nature and objectives.

What are the legal and regulatory requirements for business setup in Dubai?

Legal requirements include business license approval, trade name registration, initial approvals from relevant authorities, compliance with labor laws, and adherence to specific industry regulations. Requirements vary by business type and jurisdiction.

How do the local service agent agreement and local sponsor system work?

For mainland companies, a local service agent (for most activities) or local sponsor (for certain activities) is required. The local service agent provides administrative support without ownership rights, while a local sponsor may have ownership stakes depending on the business activity.

What are the legal structures available for business setup in Dubai, like LLC, joint stock company, etc.?

Available structures include Limited Liability Company (LLC), Free Zone Establishment (FZE), Free Zone Company (FZC), Branch Office, Representative Office, and Public/Private Joint Stock Companies. Each structure has different ownership, liability, and operational characteristics.

Can foreigners have complete foreign ownership in any business setup?

Yes, foreigners can have 100% ownership in free zones and in many mainland activities following recent UAE law changes. Certain strategic sectors may still require local partnership, but the majority of business activities now allow full foreign ownership.

How are the legal forms and legal documents managed and attested?

Legal documents require attestation from relevant authorities including notarization, Ministry of Foreign Affairs attestation, and UAE Embassy attestation in the country of origin. We manage the entire attestation process to ensure all documents meet UAE legal requirements.

How does license renewal work?

Business licenses typically require annual renewal with updated documentation, fee payments, and compliance certificates. The renewal process varies by jurisdiction and license type. We provide renewal reminders and handle the entire process to ensure continuous business operations.

What are the restrictions on foreign company branches?

Foreign company branches can only conduct activities related to their parent company's business and cannot engage in trading activities. They require a local service agent and must maintain specific capital requirements. Branch offices are suitable for representative or project-based activities.

Do I need a physical office to start a business in Dubai?

Yes, a physical office address is required for business registration. Options include traditional offices, shared workspaces, or virtual offices depending on your business needs and jurisdiction requirements. Free zones often offer flexible office solutions for startups.

How is the digital infrastructure and support from digital government regulatory authority?

The UAE has world-class digital infrastructure with government services increasingly digitized. The Digital Government provides online portals for business registration, licensing, and compliance. This streamlines processes and reduces paperwork for business setup and ongoing operations.
